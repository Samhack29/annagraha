Crop Diagnosis
Your crop has been affected by
BLACK ROT
How it affects?

It is a bacterial disease. It spreads through infected seed or through the splashing of water and worms. Very common in areas with warm and wet weather.

How to Identify?

Cauliflower plants can be infected during any growth stage and symptoms resemble nutritional deficiencies. The below are described based on the symptoms that are shown on the major plant parts ofthe main plant.

  • On Seedlings:
    Infected seedlings become yellow, drop lower leaves, and may die. Leaves may be affected on only one side of a seedling.
  • On Leaves:
    'V ' shaped yellowish patches appear on leaf margins or tip ofthe leaves.
  • On Floral Parts:
    infected plant heads remains small and its quality is reduced making it unfit for marketing.
  • On Whole Plant:
    The infected tissue turns pale green-yellow and then turns brown and dies, the veins in infected leaves, stems, and roots sometimes become black.

Control Measures

  • Control through field operations
  • Biological Control
  • Chemical Control

CAUTION

When using a chemical or a bioagent always wear protective clothing and follow the instructions on the product label, such as dosage, timing of application and pre-harvest interval.
